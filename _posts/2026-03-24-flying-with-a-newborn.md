Nobody tells you that flying with a baby is basically a logistics operation. You're not just packing for a trip — you're packing a portable version of your entire life, anticipating every possible need at 30,000 feet, and doing it all while running on broken sleep.

I flew with my daughter Emi multiple times in her first year and a half, and I took notes so you don't have to figure it out from scratch. Here's everything I actually learned.

---

## The Gear (What to Bring, What to Skip)

**The essentials:**
- **Stroller** — we carried ours on and gate checked it. Roll it all the way down the jet bridge, hand it off, and it's waiting for you when you land.
- **Car seat + car seat bag** — gate check this too. Pro tip: the car seat bag doubles as an extra bag. We stuffed ours full of diapers. Roll it down the jet bridge on the stroller.
- **Baby wearing carrier** — I baby wore Emi through TSA and during boarding. Hands-free is everything when you're juggling a boarding pass, a diaper bag, and your dignity.
- **Pack and play + sheets** — we checked this in a large bag along with our shared suitcase.
- **Portable sound machine** — non-negotiable. Plane noise is basically white noise to them, but having your own gives you control.
- **Blanket** — so many uses. Over the car seat for naps, on the floor when Emi started crawling (yes, I put my baby on the airplane floor, no regrets), tummy time in earlier months.

**What I'd skip:**
- **Portable high chair** — I took it once or twice around the one year mark and barely used it. Leave it home.
- **Baby headphones** — took them on the first trip, forgot to use them. The plane noise didn't seem to bother Emi at all.

## Packing List by Category

### Clothes
Pack day outfits plus a few extras — blowouts don't care that you're at cruising altitude. Don't forget: bibs, burp cloths, PJs, a hat, jacket, socks, and shoes.

### Diapering
- Take more diapers than you think you need. At some point Emi started pooping more than usual on planes. You've been warned.
- **Two diaper changing pads** — this was a game changer. Leave one in the diaper bag and one wherever you're staying so you're not constantly hunting for it.
- Pack an extra set of clothes in your diaper bag specifically for the plane, plus a bag for dirty clothes.
- Wipes, lotions, sunscreen (6+ months), bath soap — the usual.

### Feeding
- **Bottles** — account for how many you use per day and how much milk storage you'll need. Bring 1-2 extras if you have space.
- **Pump, charger, and two full sets of parts** — keeping parts in a ziplock or pump parts bag means you can pump on the go without getting milk everywhere.
- **Travel drying rack, brush, and soap**
- **Ceres Chill** — this is my top recommendation for transporting breastmilk. It uses regular ice to keep milk cold and doesn't need a fridge. TSA will ask you to open it for testing, which is totally fine.
- **Formula** — TSA will test it but they won't open it. No big deal.
- Lots of snacks for the plane once they're eating solids.
- Teethers, books, and toys appropriate for whatever developmental stage you're in.

## The Flying Part (The Stuff Nobody Puts in Lists)

**At TSA:**
Put all your milk, formula, and liquids in one bin if you can. It means TSA only needs to pull one tray instead of three. Hard to pull off with a baby but worth attempting.

**Boarding:**
Baby wear through the whole thing. I baby wore Emi through TSA and all the way to our seats. It keeps your hands free for boarding passes, bags, and the inevitable chaos.

**On the plane:**
Nurse or give a bottle on the way up and on the way down — the swallowing helps with ear pressure. The key is *don't start too early*. Wait until you're actually taking off, not just sitting on the tarmac. (Though sometimes they're just too hungry and you do what you gotta do.) I brought a pacifier as backup — used it maybe once.

**Luggage strategy:**
Here's how we packed it all:
- **Carry on:** stroller, Dan's backpack, my tote, one suitcase with all of Emi's stuff
- **Gate check:** car seat in car seat bag (stuffed with diapers)
- **Checked:** pack and play, one large shared suitcase for the adults

We packed Emi's stuff in the carry-on suitcase on purpose — if the checked bag gets lost, we can shop for ourselves way more easily than replacing baby gear.

**And finally:** you can always buy whatever you forgot when you get there. This was my mantra and it saved me from overpacking more than once.

---

## The Bottom Line

Flying with a baby is chaotic and exhausting and also completely doable. Emi was a trooper every single time, and honestly the trips were worth every logistical headache. The more you do it, the more you figure out your own system.
